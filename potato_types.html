10 Types of Idaho Potatoes

Russet Burbank

Light-brown skin, oval shape, off-white flesh, high starch content, fluffy texture, excellent for absorbing flavors. Perfect for baking, frying, and mashing; often used in making french fries and potato chips.

Russet Norkotah

Medium-brown skin, oblong shape, white flesh, smooth texture, retains moisture well. Great for baking, steaming, and boiling; ideal for lighter, fluffier mashed potatoes.

Yukon Gold

Thin, light-yellow skin, buttery yellow flesh, creamy texture, low starch content. Versatile; excellent for boiling, mashing, and roasting; ideal for creamy potato soups and gratins.

Red La Soda

Bright-red skin, oval shape, white flesh, waxy texture, smooth and firm. Ideal for boiling, roasting, and salads; retains shape well, making it great for potato salads and soups.

Norland

Smooth, medium-red skin, oblong shape, white flesh, firm texture. Perfect for boiling and roasting; holds its shape well in casseroles and stews.

French Fingerling

Dark-rose skin, waxy yellow flesh with a creamy and nutty flavor, elongated shape. Best when roasted or used in gourmet dishes; works well in warm potato salads and saut√©ed preparations.

Purple Peruvian

Deep purple skin and flesh, firm texture, nutty and earthy flavor, high in antioxidants. Adds vibrant color to dishes; suitable for roasting, steaming, or slicing into salads.

Cal Red

Bright-red, smooth skin, round shape, white flesh, moist and tender texture. Ideal for steaming, boiling, and adding to soups and stews; excellent for garnishing or serving whole.

Russian Banana Fingerling

Buff-yellow skin, waxy yellow flesh, firm texture, nutty and buttery flavor, small and cylindrical. Best when roasted, grilled, or used in salads; great for elegant, bite-sized presentations.

Goldrush

Light-brown skin, oblong shape, yellowish flesh, high starch, and dry texture. Excellent for baking, frying, and mashing; great for hash browns and loaded baked potatoes.
